Lesson 1: Simple Greetings

In Lesson 1, you will learn the basic greetings to initiate conversations in Toki Pona. Practice simple phrases to greet others and create a friendly atmosphere.

Examples:

  • Hello: toki!
  • Good morning: toki pona!
  • How are you?: sina pona ala?

Lesson 2: Introducing Yourself

Lesson 2 focuses on mastering the art of introducing yourself in Toki Pona. Learn how to share your name, origin, and other essential details in a concise manner.

Examples:

  • My name is: nimi mi li...
  • I am from: mi lon ma...
  • Nice to meet you: pona lukin tawa sina!

Lesson 3: Basic Vocabulary Expansion

Lesson 3 expands your basic vocabulary in Toki Pona. Discover essential words and expressions to enhance your communication skills.

Examples:

  • Numbers: wan (one), tu (two), luka (five)
  • Common Objects: tomo (house), moku (food), telo (water)
  • Expressing Likes and Dislikes: mi olin e ni (I like it), mi olin ala e ni (I don't like it)

Lesson 4: Building Simple Sentences

Lesson 4 teaches you how to build simple sentences in Toki Pona. Understand the subject-verb-object structure, negation, and asking questions.

Examples:

  • Subject-Verb-Object Structure: mi moku e kili (I eat fruit)
  • Negation: mi ken ala sona (I don't understand)
  • Asking Questions: sina tawa lon seme? (Where are you going?)

Lesson 5: Advanced Conversational Topics

Lesson 5 explores advanced conversational topics in Toki Pona. Dive deeper into expressing emotions, discussing time, giving and receiving, and talking about hobbies.

Examples:

  • Expressing Emotions: mi olin e ni (I love it)
  • Talking about Time: tenpo ni (now), tenpo kama (later)
  • Giving and Receiving: mi pana e toki tawa sina (I give you a message)
  • Discussing Hobbies: mi olin e lukin e toki (I like reading)
